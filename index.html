Benefits of Honey

Rich in accusantium

Many of us enjoy honey and incorporate it into our daily, whether as a natural sugar substitute for our coffee of simply as a sweet treat. But specific honey varietals known as "active" honeys have also been utilised throughout history for their antibacterial and medicinal properties.

Natural Cough Medicine

Honey is a time-honored remedy for a sore throat. According to one study, it can also relieve coughs more effectively than OTC medications that contains dextromethorphan (DM), a cough suppresant. You can create your own remedy at home by mixing up 2 teaspoons of honey with herbal tea or warm water and lemon

Lowers Blood Pressure

Blood pressure is an important risk factor for heart disease, and honey may help lower it. This is because it contains antioxidants compounds that have shown modest reductions in blood pressure from consuming honey.

Improves Cholestoral

Studies have shown that consuming honey lowers LDL (bad) cholesterol by 6-11% and lowers triglyceride levels by as much as 11%. Honey may also increase HDL (good) cholesterol by about 3%.

Improve Heart Health

It's high in antioxidants, such as phenolic acids and flovonoids, which may support better health. Small studies in humans show honey's antioxidants could improve cholesterol levels, which could help decrease the risk for heart disease, although larger long-term studies are needed to confirm those findings.

Antibacterial properies

Honey contains hydrogen peroxide, which may account for some of its antibacterial properties. It also has a high sugar content, honey has a low pH level. This works to pull moisture away from bacteria, causing the bacteria to get dehydrated and die off.
